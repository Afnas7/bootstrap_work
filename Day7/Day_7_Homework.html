Alappuzha

Alappuzha, also known as Alleppey, is a picturesque district in Kerala, India, famed for its extensive backwaters, canals, lagoons, and beaches. It is called the "Venice of the East" due to its unique landscape of canals and water bodies, including the Kuttanad region, which is below sea level. The district is a major tourist destination, offering houseboat cruises and being a hub for the coir industry and marine products. Geography & Topography Waterways: Alappuzha is characterized by its network of canals, rivers, and lagoons, making it a primary backwater destination in India. Coastal: The district has a significant coastline along the Arabian Sea and is mostly a sandy strip of land. Kuttanad: The Kuttanad region is notably below sea level and is known as the "Rice Bowl of Kerala" for its low-lying agricultural land.

Kovalam

Kovalam Beach is a famous, internationally recognized coastal destination in Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala, known for its three crescent-shaped beaches, calm waters, and palm-fringed surroundings. A popular spot for tourists since the 1930s, it offers beautiful scenery, diverse leisure activities, and is the ideal location for a relaxing getaway. Key Features Three Crescent Beaches: The beach features three distinct, palm-fringed coves, each offering a unique atmosphere. Calm Waters: A large rocky promontory creates a sheltered bay with tranquil waters perfect for swimming and sea bathing. Tourist Hub: Kovalam has been a favorite destination for tourists since the 1930s, attracting visitors from around the world. Proximity to Thiruvananthapuram: Located approximately 15 km from Thiruvananthapuram, the capital city, it is easily accessible

Trivandrum

Thiruvananthapuram (also known as Trivandrum) is the capital city and southernmost district of Kerala, India, renowned for its ancient temples like the Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple, and vibrant cultural heritage. The region offers a mix of attractions including famous beaches such as Kovalam, historical monuments, backwaters, and a rich cultural landscape. The city is built on seven hills and serves as a gateway to other nearby tourist destinations. Key Details Location: Situated on India's southwestern tip, stretching along the Arabian Sea coast. Capital: It is the capital of the state of Kerala and the headquarters of the Thiruvananthapuram district. History: The area is an ancient holy city with a history of over 1000 years, with the modern city established by King Marthanda Varma of the Travancore royal family in 1745.
